About the MQ-2 Sensor

The MQ-2 Gas Sensor is a multi-purpose sensor that can detect various flammable gases, including LPG, propane, methane, alcohol, hydrogen, and smoke. It's often used in gas leakage detection systems in homes and industries.

It operates on the principle of a metal oxide semiconductor (MOS). The sensor's resistance changes when it comes into contact with the target gas. This change in resistance is then converted into an analog voltage output, which can be read by a microcontroller's analog pin. Many MQ-2 modules also include a digital output that triggers when a certain gas concentration threshold is met, which can be adjusted with an onboard potentiometer.

This sensor is crucial for safety applications like gas leak alarms, fire detection, and air quality monitoring systems.
